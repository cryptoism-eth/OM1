# Multi-Agent AI Governance in Decentralized Systems

## Overview
This research explores how multi-agent artificial intelligence systems can be used as a governance layer in decentralized blockchain networks.

The core idea is to replace ad-hoc human intervention with predefined, rule-based AI agents that monitor on-chain activity, detect anomalies, and coordinate responses without relying on social consensus or centralized authority.

## Motivation
In current blockchain systems, major hacks are often visible on-chain, yet no action is taken to protect users due to decentralization and "code is law" principles.

This creates a gap between technical capability and governance restraint.

## Proposed Model
- Multiple independent AI agents monitor the network
- Agents operate under strict, predefined rules
- No single AI has authority
- Agents validate and cross-check each other
- Interventions are limited, transparent, and protocol-defined

## Benefits
- Improved security without arbitrary human decisions
- Reduced user losses during exploits
- Preservation of scalability
- Redefined, rule-based decentralization

## Conclusion
While this approach does not fully solve the blockchain trilemma, it represents a practical evolution toward safer and more predictable decentralized systems.
